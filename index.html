The idea

Loyalty shouldn't live in twenty apps.

We've all built small, scattered empires of points we never use and rewards we never see — an airline app over here, a supermarket card over there, a hotel tier we keep meaning to check.

Confluence brings them into one place, and gives them one shared language: pounds. Nothing clever. Just the honest sum of everything you've patiently earned.

Layer one · informational

The true weight of everything you've earned.

Airline miles, supermarket points, hotel nights, coffee stamps — each speaks a different dialect. Confluence reads them all and translates them to the only language that really matters: what you could spend.

What you've actually earned, in pounds, at the bottom of every ledger.

Layer two · insight

And then, the next move.

Points expire quietly. Programmes trade in their own shadows. You can turn 40,000 miles into three hotel nights; three hotel nights into a voucher; a voucher into something you'll actually use.

Confluence watches the edges for you, and tells you — calmly, once — when a move is worth making.

12,000 Avios expire in 21 days

Worth £120 now. Unused, worth nothing. A short flight to Edinburgh, or the transfer to Nectar, would keep them.

Your 9,420 Nectar points can become a £52 John Lewis voucher — £5 more than spending them at a Sainsbury's till.
